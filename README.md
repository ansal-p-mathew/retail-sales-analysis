# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Overview

This project presents an end-to-end data analytics workflow to analyze customer shopping behavior and generate actionable insights.

The analysis is structured using


* **Descriptive Analytics (What happened)**
* **Diagnostic Analytics (Why it happened)**

Using Python, SQL, and Power BI, raw transactional data is transformed into meaningful business intelligence.

---

## Business Objectives

* Understand customer purchasing patterns
* Identify key drivers of revenue and sales
* Analyze trends over time
* Segment customers based on behavior
* Enable data-driven business decisions

---

## Tech Stack

* **Python (Pandas, NumPy)** – Data cleaning & preprocessing
* **SQL** – Data analysis & querying
* **Power BI** – Dashboard creation & visualization

---

## Dataset

* **Name:** Customer Shopping Behavior Dataset
* **Type:** Transactional Data
* **Contents:** Customer demographics, product categories, purchase amounts, timestamps

---

## Project Workflow

### 🔹 Data Cleaning & Preparation (Python)

* Removed duplicate records
* Handled missing values
* Standardized column formats
* Converted data types for analysis

---

## 🔹 Descriptive Analysis (What Happened?)

Descriptive analytics was used to summarize historical customer and sales data:

### Sales & Revenue Analysis

* Total revenue generated
* Number of orders and transactions
* Monthly and yearly sales trends
* Revenue distribution across categories

### Customer Behavior Analysis

* Purchase frequency per customer
* Average order value
* Customer activity patterns over time

### Product & Category Performance

* Top-performing categories by sales
* Contribution of each category to total revenue
* Product-level sales distribution

This provides a **clear snapshot of overall business performance and customer activity**.

---

## 🔹 Diagnostic Analysis (Why It Happened?)

Diagnostic analytics was applied to identify underlying causes behind trends and patterns:

* Identification of **high-revenue categories** driving overall sales
* Detection of **low-performing categories** contributing less revenue
* Analysis of **customer segments** (high-value vs low-value customers)
* Understanding **seasonal trends** and fluctuations in purchasing behavior
* Correlation between **purchase frequency and revenue contribution**
* Identification of **customer groups driving repeat purchases**

This helps explain **why certain products, customers, or periods perform better than others**.

---

## 🔹 Data Analysis (SQL)

SQL queries were used to support both descriptive and diagnostic analysis:

* Revenue aggregation and trend analysis
* Category-wise performance comparison
* Customer purchase frequency analysis
* High-value customer identification
* Pattern detection in transaction data

---

## 🔹 Data Visualization (Power BI)

The Power BI dashboard provides interactive insights into:

* KPI Cards (Revenue, Orders, Customers)
* Sales trends over time
* Category and product performance
* Customer segmentation and behavior patterns

---

## Key Insights

* A small number of categories contribute the majority of revenue
* High-value customers have a significant impact on overall sales
* Seasonal trends influence purchasing behavior
* Certain categories underperform despite having consistent demand
* Repeat customers contribute more to revenue stability
